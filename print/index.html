Epidemiology14

  • 12.9/100k person-years
  • Incidence of acute mesenteric ischemia is increasing

Etiologies3,2

  • Arterial (65-85% cases)
    • In situ thrombosis (54% arterial)
    • Arterial embolus (31% arterial)
  • Venous (5-15% cases)
  • Non-occlusive (15-30% cases)
  • Strangulating bowel obstruction

Second look 12

  • typically occurs 24-48 hours after initial surgery
  • extent of ischemia can continue to progress after the initial surgery
  • gold standard to evaluate bowel viability
  • decision to perform second look
    • performing second look on all patients demonstrated low positive rate
    • most perform second look based on findings of first operation, with emphasis to sticking with that plan no matter the patient's operative course
  • consideration for laproscopic approach
    • minimize infection, dehiscence, hernia risk
    • physiological impact of insufflation
    • adhesions, and difficulty of view
